A technical SEO audit is the most common starting point for businesses who suspect their site has underlying issues holding back rankings. What affects audit cost:

  • Number of pages (a 100-page site vs a 100,000-page ecommerce store)
  • Number of domains or subdomains to analyse
  • Technical complexity (JavaScript rendering, hreflang, faceted navigation)
  • Whether fixes are included or just recommendations
  • Turnaround time required

What’s Included in Technical SEO?

Technical SEO covers a wide range of disciplines. A professional technical SEO engagement should include the following deliverables:

  • Full site crawl (Screaming Frog / Sitebulb) — finds broken links, redirect chains, missing tags, and orphan pages across the entire site
  • Core Web Vitals assessment (LCP, CLS, INP) — a confirmed Google ranking factor since 2021; poor scores actively suppress rankings in competitive SERPs
  • Indexation analysis — which pages Google can see and which are accidentally blocked by noindex tags, robots.txt rules, or canonicalisation issues
  • Schema markup audit and implementation — structured data for rich results including FAQ, HowTo, Product, LocalBusiness, and Article schemas
  • Mobile usability testing — Google uses mobile-first indexing; mobile rendering issues directly affect how the site is crawled and ranked
  • Page speed optimisation recommendations — image compression, render-blocking resources, server response time, caching, and CDN configuration
  • Duplicate content and canonical tag review — ensures PageRank is consolidated to the correct URLs and avoids cannibalisation
  • Hreflang audit (for multi-language or multi-region sites) — incorrect hreflang implementation is one of the most common causes of international ranking issues
  • Internal linking analysis — identifies orphan pages, distributes PageRank effectively, and improves crawl efficiency
  • XML sitemap and robots.txt review — ensures search engines can discover and access all important pages while excluding low-value URLs
  • Monthly Google Search Console monitoring — coverage errors, crawl anomalies, manual actions, and Core Web Vitals field data

When Do You Need Technical SEO?

Five situations where technical SEO is urgent and should not be delayed:

  1. After a site migration — a redesign, CMS change, or URL restructure can destroy rankings overnight. Proper pre- and post-migration technical SEO prevents traffic loss and ensures historical equity is preserved through correct redirects and canonical signals.
  2. Rankings dropped suddenly — unexplained ranking losses are often caused by accidental noindex tags, blocked crawling, a developer change that broke site architecture, or a Google algorithm update targeting technical quality signals. An audit pinpoints the cause quickly.
  3. New site launch — ensuring a new site is crawlable and indexable from day one prevents weeks of invisible downtime. Pre-launch technical SEO checks are far cheaper than remediation after a botched launch.
  4. Slow page speed — Core Web Vitals are a confirmed ranking factor. Research consistently shows that a one-second improvement in Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) significantly increases both rankings and conversion rates, particularly on mobile.
  5. Duplicate content warnings — faceted navigation, parameter URLs, and pagination are common sources of duplicate content at scale. Left unmanaged, they dilute crawl budget and split ranking signals across hundreds of near-identical URLs.

Frequently Asked Questions

For a healthy site in a stable niche, an annual audit may suffice. For sites that publish frequently or have ongoing technical complexity — such as ecommerce stores with dynamic faceted navigation or sites on actively developed platforms — monthly monitoring catches new issues before they damage rankings. A one-off audit gives you a snapshot; a retainer keeps the picture current.

Basic checks (Google Search Console, PageSpeed Insights, Bing Webmaster Tools) are accessible to anyone and worth doing. But crawl analysis at scale, Core Web Vitals deep-dives across page templates, hreflang implementation, JavaScript rendering audits, and log file analysis require specialist tools like Screaming Frog, Sitebulb, Botify, or Lumar — plus the expertise to interpret what they surface. The risk of misdiagnosis can make DIY technical SEO costlier than professional help.

Quick wins — fixing broken redirects, removing accidental noindex tags, submitting an updated sitemap — can show in rankings within days once Googlebot re-crawls the affected pages. Core Web Vitals improvements typically take 4–8 weeks to influence rankings, as Google’s field data (CrUX) rolls on a 28-day window. More structural fixes like resolving crawl budget issues on large ecommerce sites may take 2–4 months to fully reflect in rankings.
