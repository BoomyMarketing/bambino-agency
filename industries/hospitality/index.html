The Hospitality Digital Challenge

Win Bookings Without Paying Platform Commission

The hospitality industry has a complex digital marketing problem: platforms like OpenTable, TheFork, Booking.com and TripAdvisor dominate search results and charge significant commissions on every booking they deliver. The hospitality businesses that win online are those that build enough direct search visibility and brand authority to attract bookings without platform dependency.

Local SEO — ranking in Google Maps and local pack results for searches like "restaurants near me", "best Italian Manchester", or "hotel with spa Leeds" — is the single most valuable channel for driving walk-in and direct online bookings. Social media, particularly Instagram and TikTok, drives discovery and brand desire. And Google Ads can capture high-intent searches at the exact moment a diner or guest is deciding where to go.

Hospitality results (Bambino, 2025): Restaurants and hotels we work with see an average 48% increase in direct bookings within 6 months of a combined local SEO + Google Ads campaign — reducing OTA commission dependency by a median 22% year-on-year, based on 19 UK hospitality businesses.

Seasonality is a defining characteristic of hospitality marketing. A restaurant that thrives in December can struggle in January without an active marketing strategy. Effective hospitality marketing anticipates seasonal demand: building Google Ads campaigns 6–8 weeks before peak occasions (Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, Christmas, bank holidays), creating content that ranks for seasonal searches, and using email marketing to re-engage past guests before competitors do.

Review management is another critical lever. Google reviews and TripAdvisor ratings directly influence both local search rankings and consumer booking decisions. A systematic approach to generating positive reviews — at the right moment, through the right channels — combined with professional, prompt responses to negative feedback, can transform your online reputation within months.

The most commercially important shift for any hospitality business is reducing OTA dependency. Every booking that comes through Booking.com or OpenTable carries a commission cost of 15–30%. By building direct search visibility through SEO, and converting visitors through a well-designed direct booking flow, our clients consistently shift the balance from platform-dependent to direct — protecting margin and building a direct customer relationship for repeat business.

Industry Challenges

What Hospitality Businesses Are Up Against

Four structural challenges that prevent restaurants, hotels and hospitality businesses from growing profitably online — and how we solve them.

OTA Commission Dependency

Booking.com, OpenTable, TheFork and TripAdvisor take 15–30% on every reservation they deliver. Businesses that rely on platforms for the majority of their bookings are handing over a significant portion of revenue — and have no direct customer relationship to leverage for repeat business.

Highly Seasonal Demand

Hospitality demand is intensely seasonal — Christmas, Valentine's Day, summer and bank holidays create sharp peaks, while January and mid-week periods can be painfully quiet. Without agile, forward-planned marketing campaigns, businesses miss peak windows and struggle to fill covers in low season.

Review Management at Scale

Google, TripAdvisor, Yelp and platform-specific reviews all influence search rankings and consumer decisions. One unmanaged negative review can cost dozens of covers. Managing, generating, and responding to reviews across multiple platforms is time-consuming without a structured system in place.

Standing Out in a Visually-Driven Category

Hospitality is one of the most visual, Instagram-native categories. Competitors with stronger content — better food photography, more engaging video, higher-quality social presence — consistently attract more discovery traffic and brand desire. Standing out requires investment in visual identity and social strategy.
