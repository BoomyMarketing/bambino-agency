What We Do

What Is Brand Community Building?

Brand community building is the strategic process of creating a space where your customers, prospects, and advocates connect with each other — not just with your brand. Owned communities (hosted on platforms such as Slack, Circle, Discord, or Facebook Groups) are assets you control entirely, free from algorithm changes or rising ad costs. Earned communities, such as organic subreddits or brand-adjacent forums, develop organically but cannot be controlled.

A well-built owned community reduces customer acquisition cost (CAC) by generating referrals and word-of-mouth organically. It increases lifetime value (LTV) because members who feel invested in a community churn less, buy more, and upgrade more readily. Rather than renting attention on social platforms, you own the relationship — and the data. Community is not a marketing tactic; it is a long-term commercial asset.

Platforms

Community Platforms We Build

We assess your audience, goals, and budget before recommending a platform. Here are the six platforms we build most frequently for UK brands.

Slack

Best for professional B2B communities, SaaS user groups, and industry peer networks where members want a workspace-style environment.

✓ Familiar to professionals, integrates with existing tools ✓ Excellent for real-time discussion and channels ✗ Free plan hides message history; paid plan needed for scale

Circle

Ideal for membership communities, course creators, and brands that need structured Spaces, events, and content libraries in one place.

✓ Native events, courses, and member directory ✓ Clean UI with strong monetisation options ✗ Monthly platform cost; less familiar to non-tech audiences

Discord

Suited to younger, tech-savvy, gaming, creator, and crypto audiences who want real-time voice, video, and text channels.

✓ Free, highly customisable, strong moderation tools ✓ Voice and video channels built in ✗ Steeper learning curve; less suited to older B2B audiences

Facebook Groups

Strong for B2C brands with existing Facebook audiences, particularly in lifestyle, parenting, fitness, and health verticals.

✓ Low barrier to entry; members already on platform ✓ Powerful discovery via Facebook's algorithm ✗ Subject to Facebook policy changes; data ownership limited

Mighty Networks

Best for paid membership communities, coaching programmes, and brands that want to host courses, events, and a community under one roof.

✓ Native monetisation, courses, live streaming ✓ Branded mobile app available ✗ Higher platform cost; overkill for smaller communities

Tribe / Bettermode

A white-label community platform that embeds into your existing website or product, ideal for SaaS customer communities and portals.

✓ Fully branded, embedded in your product or site ✓ Strong API for custom integrations ✗ Developer resource required for deeper integrations

Common Questions

Community Building FAQs

A brand community is a group of customers, prospects, and advocates who connect with each other and your brand around shared interests, goals, or experiences. Unlike social media followers, community members interact directly with one another — not just with you. Owned communities (on platforms like Slack, Circle, or Discord) give you a direct, algorithm-free relationship with your most engaged audience, generating peer-to-peer trust, user-generated content, and product feedback that is impossible to replicate through traditional marketing alone.

The best platform depends on your audience and goals. Slack suits professional B2B or SaaS communities where members want workplace-style collaboration. Circle is ideal for course creators, coaches, and membership businesses that need structured spaces and events. Discord works well for younger, tech-savvy, or gaming-adjacent audiences. Facebook Groups remain strong for B2C brands with older demographics already active on the platform. Mighty Networks is best for monetised communities with courses and events. We assess your audience, goals, and existing tech stack before recommending a platform.

Launching a community platform takes 4–6 weeks. Reaching a critical mass of genuinely engaged members typically takes 3–6 months, depending on your existing audience size, content programme, and member acquisition strategy. The fastest-growing communities we have built reached 500 active members within 90 days using a combination of existing email lists, social channels, and a structured launch campaign. Sustained growth then follows from consistent programming and member advocacy.

A community does not replace social media — it complements it. Social media is effective for top-of-funnel awareness and driving traffic; a brand community is where your most valuable customers go deeper. The key difference is ownership: your community is an owned asset not subject to algorithm changes or rising ad costs. Members of a community typically spend 2.4x more than non-members and are significantly more likely to refer others, making community one of the highest-ROI owned marketing assets you can build.

We measure community ROI across six dimensions: Active Members (weekly and monthly), Engagement Rate (posts, replies, reactions per member), Net Promoter Score (NPS) from community vs non-community customers, Revenue Influence (purchases attributed to community members), CAC Reduction (lower paid acquisition costs as community referrals grow), and Churn Reduction (community members churning at a lower rate than non-members). We build a custom analytics dashboard that tracks all six metrics monthly alongside revenue impact.

Our service includes moderation and community management as standard. During the first 90 days, our team leads daily moderation, content posting, and member engagement while training a member of your team to take over the day-to-day if you prefer an in-house model. For clients who want fully managed ongoing support, we offer a retained moderation package. All communities receive a detailed moderation playbook so standards remain consistent regardless of who manages it.
