Our 4-Step Ecommerce SEO Process

A structured approach built around revenue-driving pages — not vanity rankings.

1

Audit

Full technical crawl of your store: indexation issues, crawl budget waste, thin content, duplicate pages from filters, broken internal links, Core Web Vitals, and competitor keyword gap analysis.

2

Common Questions

Ecommerce SEO Questions Answered

Ecommerce SEO operates at a fundamentally different scale and has unique technical challenges: optimising hundreds or thousands of product and category pages, handling duplicate content generated by filters and faceted navigation, managing canonical tags for product variants, and implementing structured data (price, availability, reviews) to earn rich results in Google. A strong homepage isn't enough — the wins live in your category and product pages.

Yes — we have deep Shopify SEO expertise. We optimise collection pages (which drive the most category traffic), implement correct product schema, handle canonical issues caused by variant URLs, improve theme speed for Core Web Vitals, and ensure Shopify's default URL structure is working in your favour. We work directly in Shopify or via a developer handoff.

Absolutely. We handle WooCommerce SEO end-to-end: Yoast or RankMath configuration, product and category schema implementation, category page content optimisation, pagination handling, site speed improvements, and fixing common WooCommerce crawl issues. We've worked with stores of all sizes on WordPress.

Thin product pages — those that rely on the manufacturer's description — are one of the biggest missed opportunities in ecommerce SEO. We enrich them with unique benefit-led product copy, customer Q&A content, feature callouts, and complete structured data (Product schema with price, availability, and reviews). This differentiates your pages from competitors using the same manufacturer text and gives Google unique, indexable content to rank.

Yes — for most online stores, category pages drive the majority of organic revenue because they target high-volume head terms. We write unique category introductions, optimise headings and meta data for target keywords, improve internal linking between related categories and products, handle faceted navigation correctly, and build category-level authority through targeted link acquisition.

Category page optimisation typically shows meaningful ranking improvements within 3–4 months. Product pages can see faster gains, particularly once structured data triggers rich results (star ratings, price snippets). Quick technical wins — fixing crawl errors, improving site speed, resolving duplicate content — often surface in Google within a few weeks of implementation.

Yes. We regularly work alongside paid teams and in-house Google Shopping managers. Organic and paid search are complementary: strong organic rankings reduce your cost-per-click, improve overall search visibility, and ensure consistent messaging across channels. We coordinate to make sure product titles, descriptions, and landing pages serve both organic rankings and paid quality scores.
