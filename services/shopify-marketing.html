What We Do

What Is Shopify Marketing?

Shopify marketing is the multi-channel discipline of driving qualified traffic to your Shopify store, converting that traffic into customers, and then increasing the lifetime value of those customers through email, retention, and loyalty strategies. Unlike platform-specific marketing (such as Amazon), Shopify marketing puts your brand at the centre — building an audience and customer base that you own and can market to independently.

An effective Shopify marketing strategy is never built on a single channel. The UK’s most successful Shopify stores combine paid acquisition (Google Shopping, Meta Ads, TikTok) with owned channels (email via Klaviyo, SEO) and conversion optimisation (CRO) to create a diversified, resilient revenue engine. Each channel feeds the others: paid traffic builds your email list, email flows recover abandoned carts, SEO reduces dependence on paid spend, and CRO makes every channel more profitable.

Bambino’s Shopify marketing team manages the full funnel — from first click to repeat purchase — using a channel strategy tailored to your specific product category, average order value, and growth stage. We work with Shopify stores across all UK categories, from fashion and beauty to food & drink, sports, and home.

Bambino client data (2025): UK Shopify stores on our full marketing programme achieved an average blended ROAS of 3.4x across paid channels, with email (Klaviyo) contributing an additional 25–35% of total store revenue from flows alone. n=18 stores.

Conversion Optimisation

Shopify CRO: Convert More of Your Existing Traffic

Driving traffic to your Shopify store is only half the job. The average UK ecommerce conversion rate sits at 2–4% — meaning 96% of visitors leave without buying. Shopify CRO is the discipline of systematically improving the percentage of visitors who complete a purchase, using data rather than guesswork.

Our Shopify CRO programme uses heatmaps, session recordings, and A/B testing to identify and fix the specific elements of your store that are costing you sales. See our dedicated CRO service →

Product Page Optimisation

Above-the-fold layout, product imagery sequencing, social proof placement (reviews, UGC), benefit-led copy, size guides, and trust signals that reduce bounce rate and increase add-to-cart rate on your highest-traffic product pages.

Cart Abandonment Reduction

Identify and fix the friction points causing cart abandonment — unexpected shipping costs, trust concerns, or slow page load — then complement on-site fixes with Klaviyo abandoned cart email flows to recover lost revenue automatically.

Checkout Optimisation

Shopify’s native checkout is strong, but cart-to-purchase drop-off still averages 60–70% on UK stores. We test express checkout options, trust signals, delivery messaging, and post-purchase upsell flows to reduce drop-off at each checkout step.

Page Speed & Core Web Vitals

A 1-second improvement in Shopify page load time increases conversion rate by 7–11% on average. We audit and improve Shopify speed through image optimisation, app reduction, lazy loading, and theme code improvements.

Platform Comparison

Shopify vs WooCommerce vs Magento: Which Platform Is Right for Your UK Store?

Choosing the right ecommerce platform is the foundation of your marketing strategy. The platform you’re on affects your site speed, SEO capabilities, integration options, and development costs — all of which directly impact marketing ROI. Here’s an honest comparison for UK SME retailers.

Criteria Shopify Our Recommendation WooCommerce Magento (Adobe Commerce)
Ease of Use Excellent. No hosting or server management required. Moderate. Requires WordPress knowledge and hosting management. Complex. Requires dedicated developer resource.
Monthly Cost £25–£299/mo (Shopify plans). Predictable costs. Low base cost, but hosting, plugins, and dev time add up. High. Enterprise licences + significant dev costs.
SEO Capability Good, with known limitations (URL structure, duplicate content). Manageable with correct setup. Excellent with correct WordPress plugin setup (Yoast, RankMath). Excellent, with full URL and technical control.
Speed & Performance Fast. Shopify’s CDN and infrastructure is enterprise-grade. Variable. Depends heavily on hosting quality and plugin load. Can be very fast with proper optimisation. Expensive to achieve.
App Ecosystem 8,000+ apps. Klaviyo, Gorgias, ReCharge, Yotpo all native integrations. Large plugin library, but quality varies significantly. Extensive, but integrations often require custom development.
Best For UK SME retailers, DTC brands, fast-growing ecommerce businesses up to £50M revenue. Businesses already on WordPress, or needing heavy content integration. Large enterprises with complex catalogues and custom requirements.

Our verdict: For the vast majority of UK SME retailers, Shopify is the optimal platform for marketing performance. Its predictable costs, fast infrastructure, strong app ecosystem (particularly Klaviyo and Gorgias), and continuous investment in native selling features make it the best foundation for a multi-channel UK ecommerce marketing strategy.
