What Is SMS Marketing?

SMS marketing (also known as text message marketing) is the practice of sending promotional or transactional messages directly to customers’ mobile phones via SMS or MMS. Unlike social media or email, SMS lands directly in the native messages app — with no algorithm, no spam folder, and no competition for attention in a crowded feed.

For UK businesses, SMS is a particularly powerful channel because over 93% of UK adults own a smartphone and UK consumers check their phones over 50 times per day on average. When combined with strong personalisation and smart segmentation, SMS delivers open rates of 98%, click rates of 19–45%, and revenue-per-message that consistently outperforms email on a like-for-like send basis.

Bambino manages the entire SMS marketing lifecycle: compliance, list building, platform setup, campaign planning, personalisation, send optimisation, and reporting — so you can generate results without the legal and technical complexity of running it in-house.

Legal & Compliance

UK PECR + GDPR Compliance

SMS marketing in the UK is regulated by PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations) and UK GDPR. Non-compliance is one of the most common reasons UK businesses face ICO investigations — and fines of up to £500,000. Bambino builds compliance into the foundation of every SMS programme we run.

Explicit consent required. You must obtain clear, specific consent for SMS marketing before sending. Pre-ticked boxes, bundled consent, and vague privacy policy opt-ins are not sufficient under PECR.
Soft opt-in for existing customers. You may send marketing SMS to existing customers without fresh consent if you sold them similar products or services, offered them a clear opt-out at the time, and continue to provide an easy opt-out in every message.
ICO rules on identification. Every marketing SMS must clearly identify the sender. Anonymous or misleading sender IDs are prohibited and can result in regulatory action.
Opt-out must be simple and free. Every SMS message must include a clear, functional opt-out mechanism (typically “Reply STOP to unsubscribe”), and opt-outs must be processed immediately. There can be no charge for opting out.
Record-keeping requirements. You must maintain clear records of how and when consent was obtained, what subscribers were told, and all opt-outs. Bambino maintains audit-ready consent records for every client on our SMS programme.
ICO fines for non-compliance. The ICO can issue fines of up to £500,000 for serious PECR breaches and has increasingly targeted SMS marketing violations. In 2024 alone, the ICO issued fines totalling over £4 million to UK businesses for unlawful electronic marketing.

SMS Marketing FAQs

Yes — SMS marketing is legal in the UK provided you comply with PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations) and UK GDPR. This means obtaining explicit prior consent from recipients before sending marketing texts, providing a clear opt-out mechanism in every message, and keeping records of consent. The ICO (Information Commissioner’s Office) enforces these rules and can issue fines of up to £500,000 for serious breaches. Bambino handles all compliance requirements as part of every SMS programme.

Under PECR, you generally need explicit, freely given, informed, and unambiguous consent before sending marketing SMS messages to individuals. The consent must be specific to SMS marketing — a general privacy policy tick-box is not sufficient. There is a narrow ‘soft opt-in’ exception for existing customers: you can market similar products or services via SMS to customers who purchased from you and did not opt out, provided you gave them a clear opportunity to object at the time of purchase and in every subsequent message.

A healthy SMS opt-in rate depends on the collection method. Pop-up forms on ecommerce sites typically convert at 2–5% of visitors. Checkout opt-ins (where the customer is already engaged) often reach 10–20%. Post-purchase follow-up sequences with an SMS opt-in CTA can achieve 8–15%. For B2C businesses, a well-optimised SMS list-building strategy should grow your subscriber base at 3–8% of total email list size per month. Bambino provides a full list-building strategy as part of every SMS engagement.

SMS to business contacts (corporate subscribers) operates under different rules under PECR. You do not require prior consent to send B2B marketing SMS to corporate subscribers — a limited company, LLP, or Scottish partnership — provided you give them a clear opt-out and honour it promptly. However, sole traders and partnerships in England, Wales, and Northern Ireland are treated as individuals and require consent. In practice, most B2B SMS marketing focuses on existing client relationships, appointment reminders, and event notifications rather than cold outreach.

The most effective UK SMS list-building tactics include: website pop-ups with an incentive (discount, free shipping, early access), checkout opt-in checkboxes, post-purchase email sequences with an SMS opt-in CTA, social media lead generation ads, in-store sign-up sheets, and QR codes on packaging or receipts. A standard SMS is 160 characters using the GSM-7 character set. Messages longer than 160 characters are split into segments of 153 characters each (to accommodate header data linking the segments) and billed accordingly — a 320-character message counts as two SMS messages. If you use special characters outside the GSM-7 set (such as curly quotes, emoji, or certain accented characters), the message switches to Unicode encoding, reducing the single-message limit to 70 characters. We always review message copy for character efficiency to control costs.
