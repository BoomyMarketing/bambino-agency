What Is Webflow?

Webflow: Visual Design, Clean Code

Webflow is a visual web development platform that generates clean, production-quality HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — without the compromises of traditional website builders. Unlike Wix or Squarespace, Webflow gives designers and developers complete visual control over every element on the page, producing bespoke designs that look exactly as intended on every screen size without template constraints.

Webflow includes a powerful CMS (Content Management System) for managing blogs, case studies, team members, and any other dynamic content — all editable by non-technical team members through Webflow’s intuitive Editor. It also includes built-in hosting on AWS CloudFront, meaning there are no hosting providers to manage, no WordPress plugins to update, and no security patches to apply. The result is a website that simply works — fast, secure, and maintainable.

For UK businesses tired of WordPress maintenance, slow load speeds, and agencies charging monthly retainers just to keep the lights on, Webflow represents a genuinely better way to own a website. The code is clean, the CMS is elegant, and the editorial experience is so straightforward that your marketing team will actually use it.

Bambino Webflow clients: Across our UK Webflow builds, the average Google PageSpeed (mobile) score achieved is 87/100 at launch — compared to an average of 54/100 for the WordPress sites we have migrated from. No plugins. No bloat. Just fast.

How We Work

Our Webflow Build Process

A structured five-phase process that takes you from project brief to a live, SEO-configured, editable Webflow website — in an average of four weeks for standard builds.

1

Discovery

We run a structured kick-off workshop covering your brand, target audience, content structure, SEO requirements, CMS needs, and integrations. We produce a scoped sitemap and project timeline before any design work begins.

2

Design

Wireframes establish the structure and layout logic; then our designers produce high-fidelity visual designs in Figma for desktop and mobile. We present two design routes and iterate based on your feedback before locking designs for development.

3

Development

We build the approved designs in Webflow — responsive across all screen sizes, with clean class structure, CMS integration, custom interactions, and all third-party scripts and integrations configured. Content is populated during this phase.

4

QA

Cross-browser and cross-device testing (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge; iOS and Android), SEO configuration audit, form testing, link checking, Google PageSpeed measurement, and a pre-launch checklist review before any DNS changes are made.

5

Launch

DNS cutover with minimal downtime, 301 redirect verification, GA4 and Google Search Console setup, post-launch QA, and structured team training covering Webflow Editor, CMS publishing, and day-to-day content management tasks.

Webflow Enterprise vs Workspace

Webflow offers different plan tiers suited to different business sizes and requirements. Here’s how to choose the right Webflow plan for your organisation.

For Most Businesses

Webflow Site Plans

For individual websites — covering most UK business, agency, and ecommerce use cases. Hosted on Webflow’s infrastructure with your custom domain, SSL, and Webflow CMS included.

  • Basic plan: ~£14/month (static site, no CMS)
  • CMS plan: ~£19/month (blog, dynamic pages up to 2,000 CMS items)
  • Business plan: ~£36/month (up to 10,000 CMS items, more form submissions)
  • E-commerce plans: from ~£29/month for Webflow Ecommerce
  • Ideal for: SMEs, startups, professional services, ecommerce stores with smaller catalogues
For Larger Organisations

Webflow Enterprise

Webflow Enterprise is designed for large organisations with multiple team members, advanced security requirements, SSO, SLA guarantees, and dedicated support — typically from ~£1,500/month.

  • SOC 2 Type II compliance and enterprise SSO
  • Guaranteed 99.99% uptime SLA
  • Advanced user permissions and publishing workflows
  • Custom contract and invoicing terms
  • Dedicated customer success and priority support
  • Ideal for: large enterprises, regulated industries, multi-brand organisations, global sites
