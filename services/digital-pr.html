How It Works

Our 4-Step Digital PR Process

From story ideation to national press coverage — a proven methodology for earning high-authority backlinks.

1

Ideation & Research

We research trending topics in your sector, identify data gaps that journalists want filled, and develop original campaign concepts backed by surveys, proprietary data, or analysis of publicly available datasets.

2

Story Development

We turn research into a fully packaged press release, data visualisations, and supporting assets — crafted to meet the specific editorial standards and interests of target publications, not just sent generically to a press list.

3

Journalist Outreach

We pitch to relevant journalists and editors using personalised outreach built on genuine relationships. We target national press, regional media, and sector trade publications based on where your audience reads and where backlinks carry most SEO value.

4

Coverage & Reporting

We track every placement secured, providing monthly coverage reports that include publication name, DA, estimated traffic, and link equity value. We also monitor for unlinked mentions and secure link insertions where possible.

Common Questions

Digital PR FAQs

Digital PR focuses on earning online coverage and backlinks from authoritative publications — The Guardian, BBC, Forbes, industry trade press — with the primary goal of improving SEO rankings and domain authority alongside brand visibility. Traditional PR focuses primarily on brand awareness through print and broadcast media. Digital PR campaigns are built around shareable data, stories, and research that journalists want to publish online.

Traditional link building often involves outreach to smaller, less authoritative sites — securing links through guest posts, directories, or content exchanges. Digital PR earns links from national press and high-authority publications (DA 60+) through genuinely newsworthy stories and data. These links carry significantly more SEO value and also drive direct referral traffic and brand authority that link building alone cannot replicate.

We target national UK publications including The Guardian, The Times, BBC, The Telegraph, Mail Online, The Independent, and Forbes UK, alongside sector-specific trade publications relevant to your industry. Target publication lists are agreed with you during campaign planning and are tailored to where your audience actually reads, not just the highest domain authority sites.

Individual campaigns typically see coverage within 4–8 weeks of launch, depending on the story’s news value and journalist response. SEO benefits — improved domain authority and ranking improvements — typically become visible within 3–6 months of securing high-authority coverage. Our clients see an average domain rating improvement of 7 points within 6 months.

We measure digital PR success through number of placements secured, domain authority of referring publications, estimated referral traffic value, link equity (DA and traffic of linking domains), and domain rating improvement over time. We also track keyword ranking improvements in target categories. Monthly reports include a full coverage report with all placements, their DA, and estimated traffic value.

Digital PR can replace the need for lower-quality link building, but it works best as part of a broader SEO strategy that includes technical SEO, on-page optimisation, and content marketing. For clients with strong existing domain authority, digital PR is often the highest-ROI activity for accelerating ranking improvements. For newer sites, a combination of digital PR and targeted link building may be more appropriate.

We run digital PR campaigns for UK businesses across finance, property, legal, health and wellness, ecommerce and retail, technology and SaaS, hospitality, and consumer brands. The best digital PR stories are often data-led — surveys, proprietary research, and analysis of publicly available data — which we can develop for most industries.
