What Is Amazon Marketing?

Amazon marketing is the combination of strategies used to increase a product’s visibility, click-through rate, and conversion rate on Amazon’s marketplace. Unlike a single-channel approach, effective Amazon marketing integrates two complementary disciplines: Amazon PPC (paid advertising through Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, and Sponsored Display) and Amazon SEO (organic ranking through keyword-optimised titles, bullet points, backend search terms, and strong sales velocity).

PPC drives immediate traffic and revenue while your organic ranking builds. SEO lowers your dependence on paid advertising over time as your listings climb the organic results. The two work in a virtuous cycle: PPC sales improve your BSR (Best Seller Rank), which in turn improves your organic position, which reduces the amount of paid spend required to maintain your sales volume.

For UK sellers, Amazon marketing also involves navigating Amazon’s regional nuances — UK-specific search behaviour, British spelling in listing copy, VAT compliance, FBA logistics, and competition from both domestic and European sellers. Our team operates exclusively in the UK market and understands the category landscape, seasonal demand patterns, and buyer psychology specific to Amazon.co.uk.

Brand Protection

Amazon Brand Registry for UK Sellers

Amazon Brand Registry is a programme that gives brand owners greater control over their product listings and access to a suite of powerful marketing tools unavailable to unregistered sellers. If you own a registered trademark in the UK (or another accepted country) and sell on Amazon, Brand Registry is one of the most valuable steps you can take for your Amazon business.

How to Qualify for Amazon Brand Registry

To enrol in Brand Registry, you need: a registered trademark in the UK (or another accepted country), a brand logo that matches the trademark, images of your products or packaging displaying the brand name, and an active Amazon Seller Central or Vendor Central account. If your trademark is still pending, Amazon’s IP Accelerator programme can connect you with approved UK trademark attorneys and grant provisional Brand Registry access while your application is processed.

Key Metrics

ACOS vs TACoS: Understanding Your Amazon Ad Metrics

Two metrics dominate Amazon advertising performance reporting: ACOS and TACoS. Understanding the difference — and knowing which to optimise for — is fundamental to profitable Amazon marketing in the UK.

ACOS

Advertising Cost of Sale

Formula: (Ad Spend ÷ Ad Revenue) × 100

ACOS measures the efficiency of your paid campaigns in isolation — how much you spend in ads for every £1 of revenue those ads directly generate. A 25% ACOS means you spent £25 to generate £100 in attributed sales. Lower ACOS = more efficient advertising.

TACoS

Total Advertising Cost of Sale

Formula: (Ad Spend ÷ Total Revenue) × 100

TACoS measures ad spend against all of your Amazon revenue — both ad-attributed and organic. A falling TACoS over time signals that your organic ranking is growing, reducing your dependence on paid advertising. This is the North Star metric for long-term Amazon profitability.
