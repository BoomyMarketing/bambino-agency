Local SEO Questions Answered

Local SEO is the process of optimising your online presence to attract more business from relevant local searches. It includes optimising your Google Business Profile, building consistent local citations across directories, targeting 'near me' and city-specific keywords, and earning strong reviews — all to improve your visibility in the local pack (the map results) and nearby organic rankings.

Google Business Profile improvements — such as better ranking in the map pack — can be seen within a few weeks of optimisation. Broader local organic rankings typically improve within 2–4 months for most local search terms. More competitive markets or multi-location businesses may take a little longer, but we'll set realistic expectations from day one.

We handle everything: profile setup and optimisation, regular photo uploads, weekly Google Posts, responding to reviews professionally, managing the Q&A section, and monitoring for spam or incorrect edits. A well-managed GBP is often the single biggest driver of local calls and footfall.

Local SEO targets geographic searches — 'near me' queries, city-specific terms like 'plumber Manchester', and Google Maps results. National SEO targets broader, non-location-specific keywords that reach audiences across the whole UK. Both require different strategies: local SEO relies heavily on GBP, NAP consistency, and local authority signals, while national SEO focuses more on domain authority and content depth. Many UK businesses benefit from a combination of both.
